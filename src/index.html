Henry Ford

Here's a time line of Henry Ford life:

  • 1863 born in Springwells Township, Michigan
  • 1879 - Left home to work as an apprentice machinist in Detroit, first with James F. Flower & Bros., and later with the Detroit Dry Dock Co
  • 1882 - Returned to Dearborn to work on the family farm, where he became adept at operating the Westinghouse portable steam engine
  • 1891 - Became an engineer with the Edison Illuminating Company of Detroit
  • 1896 - Сreated a self-propelled vehicle, which he named the Ford Quadricycle
  • 1898 - Designed and built a second vehicle
  • 1899 - Resigned from the Edison Company and founded the Detroit Automobile Company
  • 1901 - The company was not successful and was dissolved
  • 1902 - H. Ford & A. Malcomson formed a partnership, "Ford & Malcomson, Ltd"
  • 1903 - Ford & Malcomson was reincorporated as the Ford Motor Company
  • 1908 - Debuted the Model T
  • 1913 - Introduced moving assembly belts into his plants, which enabled an enormous increase in production
  • 1916 - As the price dropped to $360 for the basic touring car, sales reached 472,000
  • 1918 - Half of all cars in the United States were Model Ts
  • 1927 - Created the Model A
  • 1932 - Dropped a bombshell with the flathead Ford V8, the first low-price eight-cylinder engine
  • 1939 - Ford launched Mercury as a mid-range make to challenge Dodge and Buick
  • 1945 - His health failing, Ford ceded the company Presidency to his grandson, Henry Ford II, and retired
  • 1947 - dies at the age of 83.
